The problem we solve

AI strategy breaks down in the messy middle.

Most organisations have ambition, tools, and pressure to move. The hard part is turning that energy into accountable systems that can survive architecture, risk, procurement, and operations.

AI pilots stall

Teams identify promising use cases, then get stuck on ownership, approval paths, data readiness, and what needs to be true before production.

Legacy systems resist change

Existing platforms, APIs, data flows, and manual processes were not designed for modern AI workflows. Integration decides whether ideas ship.

Boards demand accountability

Leaders need speed, but also evidence: decision rights, controls, audit paths, and clear human accountability when AI affects real outcomes.

Founder Bio

Morgane Oger

Morgane Oger founded RO IT Systems in 1999 in Sheffield, UK. The company later operated through Zurich and Vancouver, building experience across financial services, utilities, aviation, and public-interest technology.

Earlier in her career, Morgane worked in autonomous systems and subsea robotics—environments where automation limits and safety margins are real engineering problems. That grounding now shapes her approach to AI governance and agentic systems.

She brings more than two decades in enterprise data platforms, cloud architecture, systems integration, and AI governance—spanning engineering, executive technology leadership, and delivery. Recipient of Canada’s Meritorious Service Medal.
